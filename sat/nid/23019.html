CORONAS-I is the first satellite of the CORONAS group. CORONAS-I was launched on March 2, 1992 from the Plesetsk Test Range (Russia). CORONAS-I is a Sun-oriented satellite orbiting at 500km altitude and 82 degrees inclination. CORONAS-I data is available from March 1994 to October 1994.

CORONAS-I carries SCR (Solar Cosmic Rays) instrument package created in D.V. Skobeltsyn Institute of Nuclear Physics of the Moscow State University (SINP MSU). SCR consists of three instruments: MCR (Monitor of Cosmic Rays), SONG (Solar Neutron and Gamma-Quantum) and SCR-3 (Solar Cosmic Rays-3). SCR package onboard CORONAS-I measures energetic electrons of energy E>0.5 MeV and energetic protons from ~1 MeV to hundreds of MeV and the nuclei from He to O covering the energy range from units to tenths MeV/nucleon. The SCR combines some of the most sensitive energetic particle sensors (MCR, SONG) ever flown in space.

The experimental data in the database is accompanied by geomagnetic coordinates (L, B, magnetic latitude and longitude). The geomagnetic field is calculated using the IGRF95 model.

Solar Neutrons and Gamma rays (SONG)

Dr. S.P. Ryumin

The main detector is a large scintillation crystal CsI(Tl) with diameter 20 cm and height 10 cm. The effective geometric factor for detecting of electrons with energy >55 MeV and protons with energy >80 MeV is about 4000 cm2×sr. The detector is overloaded in the region of South Atlantic Anomaly and in the maximum of outer electron radiation belt where the fluxes of charged particles is extremely high.

Solar Cosmic Rays-3 (SCR-3)

Dr. A.N. Podorolsky

SCR-3 has standard semiconductor telescope consisting of four axial n-p detectors with increasing thickness: 25, 100, 2000 and 2000 mm. SCR-3 measured the elemental composition from He to O covering the energy range from 1.5 to 10 MeV/nucleon for He, and from 3.3 to 42 MeV/nucleon for O. The geometric factor of telescope is 0.2 cm2×sr.

Monitor of Cosmic Rays (MCR)

Dr. Yu.V. Gotseluk

The Detectors of MCR device were oriented along the Earth-Sun axis.Charged particles were detected by two detector blocks:

  1. Plastic scintillating hemisphere + semiconductor thin n-p detector
  2. Telescope: 3 semiconductor n-p detectors + CsI scintillating crystal in plastic anticoincidence shield
